Gabriel O. Shonaike, Ph.D., C.Eng. (COREN) –DATA AND RESEARCH

Gabriel Obafemi Shonaike is a Professor of Polymer Engineering in the Department of Chemical and Polymer Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, Lagos State University (LASU), Epe Campus. He graduated with first class at the University of Abertay, Dundee Scotland in 1979 and thereafter moved to Strathclyde University, Glasgow, Scotland. In 1986, however, he bagged his PhD in Materials Science at University of London.

Gabriel joined Faculty of Engineering Technology and Environmental Science (FETES), Lagos State University in Dec 1986. He became the first African on a long-term Japan Society for the Promotion of Science postdoctoral fellow in 1988 and also the first African on Inoue Foundation for Science postdoctoral fellow in 1989. In the late 1989, he returned to LASU but left in 1990 to join Omron Corporation, Kyoto, Japan. in 1992, he joined Kyoto Institute of Technology as an Assistant Professor and was promoted to Associate Professor in 1994. Kyoto Institute of Technology is the Centre for fibre reinforced composite in Asia and was part of the team that carried out various research activities in the in areas of fibre reinforced composites and polymer blends. Professor Shonaike’s advent to the corporate world took him to Nippon Ghosei, Kurashiki, Japan, as an Advisor and Senior scientist, respectively here, he also led a team of researchers that worked on barrier polymers for various applications in medical, food and construction industries. He moved to Noltex USA (owned by Nippon Ghosei) in 2002 transferring technology from the parent company in Japan. He later worked on the development of carbon fiber reinforced materials for microchips at Intel Corporation, Arizona in 2004. Gabriel moved to Monsanto (Solutia) in 2006 and then DuPont (Invista) as a Group Leader and Principal Scientist, respectively.

Prof. Shonaike, an erudite scholar and researcher has authored/co-authored over 150 scientific papers in academic journals and peer refereed conference presentations. He also has to his credit about half a dozen invited Book Chapters and has coedited two books, including (Polymer Alloys and Blends, Mercel Dekker, NY 1999 (736 pages) and Advanced PolymericMaterials-Structure.
